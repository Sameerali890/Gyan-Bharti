The National Defence Academy (NDA) exam is conducted by UPSC for recruitment into the Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force. Candidates must have completed 10+2, with Physics and Mathematics required for the Air Force and Navy. The selection process includes a written exam, followed by the Service Selection Board (SSB) interview and medical examination. NDA cadets undergo three years of training at the NDA, followed by specialized training at respective academies. Defence jobs in India include roles in the Army, Navy, Air Force, and paramilitary forces. Recruitment is conducted through exams like NDA, CDS, AFCAT, and Agniveer schemes. Technical entries require engineering backgrounds, while non-technical roles require a bachelor's degree or 10+2 qualification. Defence personnel receive excellent salaries, allowances, and benefits like housing, medical facilities, and pension schemes. Career growth opportunities are vast, leading to higher ranks through promotions and experience. The Assistant Commandant (AC) post in the Central Armed Police Forces (CAPF) is a Group A officer position responsible for leading paramilitary forces like BSF, CRPF, CISF, ITBP, and SSB. The syllabus for NDA and AC exams includes Mathematics, General Knowledge, English, and Reasoning. Physical fitness is a key requirement for all defence and paramilitary jobs, with specific criteria for height, weight, and endurance. Defence personnel are trained in combat, leadership, and strategic operations. Their role is to protect national security, maintain law and order, and assist in disaster relief operations. Serving in defence and paramilitary forces is a prestigious and challenging career that demands discipline, bravery, and dedication. Officers enjoy a high social status, adventure-filled careers, and opportunities to serve in international peacekeeping missions. Defence jobs provide long-term security, post-retirement benefits, and a strong sense of patriotism and duty.
